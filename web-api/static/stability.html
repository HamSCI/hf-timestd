What is Allan Deviation?

Allan deviation (ADEV) measures the frequency stability of an oscillator or timing source as a function of averaging time τ. Unlike standard deviation, ADEV converges for common noise types found in oscillators.

σy(τ) = √[ ½ ⟨(ȳn+1 - ȳn)²⟩ ]

Noise Types

τ+1 Random Walk FM (drift)
τ0 Flicker FM (1/f noise)
τ-½ White FM (averages down)
τ-1 Flicker PM

Interpretation

The slope of the ADEV plot on log-log axes reveals the dominant noise type. For HF timing, we expect:

  • Short τ: White noise from measurement
  • Medium τ: Flicker noise from ionosphere
  • Long τ: Possible drift or diurnal effects
